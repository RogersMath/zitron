How to Argue With An AI Booster

Arguments Presented in Ed Zitron's Three-Part Series

# AI Booster Argument Ed Zitron's Counter-Argument
EPISODE 1 - DENIAL STAGE
Watch Part 1
1 You're just being a hater for attention / contrarians just do it for clicks Being an AI booster is more lucrative - you get panel invites, access to executives, and headlines. Being a critic requires constant justification while boosters never have to explain themselves. Ask them to explain how criticism monetizes better than promoting what the rich and powerful want reported.
2 You just don't get it It's peculiar to suggest consumers must justify themselves to products. If I don't get it, it's the booster's job to explain why using their own experience, not anecdotes. Ask what specific use cases are transformative based on their direct experience.
3 AI is powerful and getting exponentially more powerful Ask them to define "powerful." If they mention benchmarks, ask how those apply to real-world scenarios. If they mention reasoning, ask for plain English explanations of use cases this enables, not just how it works technically.
4 AI will [do various future things] Tell them to stop using "will" and explain what AI can do now. If they insist on future predictions, ask for specific dates and hold them accountable to those timelines.
5 Agents will automate large parts of our economy Agents don't work - they're just workflows where LLMs make decisions like web searching. Ask them to point to actual autonomous agents. Salesforce research shows only 58% success on single-step tasks, 35% on multi-step tasks. OpenAI reduced agent projections by $26 billion.
6 AI is like the early days of the internet One person being wrong about the internet doesn't validate AI. Early internet costs were tiny compared to AI's massive power grid expansion needs. The "early days" comparison is historically inaccurate.
7 People said smartphones wouldn't be big This is a lie. The iPhone was immediately successful, selling 4 million units in 6 months on an exclusive carrier. There were accurate roadmaps for smartphones in the early 2000s, but no such roadmap exists for generative AI.
8 We're in the early days of AI We're not early. The transformer paper was published in 2017, ChatGPT launched in 2022. 79% of Americans have heard of ChatGPT, 34% have used it. 33% of global VC funding went to AI in 2024. These companies have unlimited resources and attention but still can't create meaningful use cases.
EPISODE 2 - BARGAINING STAGE
Watch Part 2
9 This is just like the dot-com boom - the infrastructure will be useful later The fiber boom was fundamentally different. GPUs are depreciating assets already available cheaply on eBay. Unlike fiber optic cables with varied uses, AI GPUs are limited by CUDA and designed for one specific function. This wasn't an infrastructure buildout but a centralized asset bubble.
10 AI 2027 predictions show exponential improvement coming AI 2027 is fanfiction written to scare people. It's speculative fiction with no proof that self-learning agents are possible. Nobody has done it and nobody will do it. It's a marketing device using "spectral evidence" like Salem witch trials.
11 The cost of inference is coming down This is wrong. The cost of inference has actually gone up about 10x over the past 2 years. While token prices may drop for some models, reasoning models burn significantly more tokens. New models cost the same and require more computation, increasing overall costs.
12 OpenAI and Anthropic are like Uber - they burned money but became profitable Uber was immediately useful and replaced a genuinely bad system (calling cabs). OpenAI and Anthropic have each burned more than 4x what Uber burned in its entire existence, just since 2024. Their infrastructure costs are paid by Microsoft/Amazon. True cost is 10x what Uber burned.
13 AI is becoming essential to government and enterprise Government contracts are low-cost trials designed to create "too big to fail" perception. Having cheap access doesn't make something essential or useful. What essential experience would disappear if ChatGPT vanished? What specific outcomes justify the costs?
14 Data centers drive economic growth and innovation These data centers aren't driving economic growth beyond construction costs. There's maybe $40 billion in AI revenue with no profit. No great loss would occur from the death of large language models.
EPISODE 3 - FINAL ARGUMENTS
Watch Part 3
15 AI is like Amazon Web Services - took time to be profitable AWS was created out of necessity, had proven demand, and was break-even within 3 years. It improved something people already did. OpenAI was founded in 2015 and still isn't profitable. AWS burned about 10% of what AI companies have burned.
16 High annualized revenues prove success Annualized revenue is monthly revenue times 12 - a misleading metric that doesn't account for churn. If they mention annualized numbers, ask about actual profit and burn rate.
17 AI companies are in growth mode, they'll pull the profit lever when ready Why have none of them done this? Not one AI company has demonstrated the ability to become profitable.
18 AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) is coming We don't know how thinking works in humans and can't extrapolate to machines. LLMs cannot re-evaluate or learn. Even Meta's chief AI scientist says AGI isn't possible with transformer models. Anyone claiming to know how to achieve AGI is lying.
19 Industry insiders say powerful secret models exist This is like claiming your uncle works at Nintendo. Companies raise billions with no product roadmap because they see grifting opportunities. Anyone with "secret" capabilities isn't talking about it because it doesn't exist.
20 ChatGPT has 700 million weekly users - popularity proves utility OpenAI won't define "active user" and has sustained the biggest PR campaign outside of presidencies. People were pressured to try the service under false pretenses. High usage numbers don't prove meaningful utility - conversion to paid subscribers is mediocre at 3.5%.
21 OpenAI is making tons of money ($1 billion monthly revenue) Enterprise adoption is high but transformation is low per MIT study. Companies aren't seeing returns. OpenAI's growth will slow because actual returns aren't there. Also burning $10+ billion yearly with unclear path to profitability.
22 Personal anecdotes - I use AI and love it Individual use cases don't matter for assessing transformational technology claims. Common uses (brainstorming, search replacement, research) aren't impressive business models and don't justify the massive infrastructure investment.
23 Vibe coding allows non-programmers to build software While technically possible to type "build me a website," the results aren't functional, secure, or useful. Show me one fully functional company built by someone who can't read or write code. Studies suggest AI coding environments make software engineers 19% slower.
24 AI is replacing jobs, especially for young people No proof AI is causing job displacement. Reports cite correlations without causation. For example, accounting job declines are due to fewer people becoming CPAs, not AI replacement. Amazon's cloud chief called replacing junior employees with AI "one of the dumbest things" he's heard.
25 AI is advancing scientific research Claims about AI discovering materials have been withdrawn due to integrity concerns. Foundation models are insufficient for scientific research. LLMs can't do actual scientific research despite claims otherwise.
